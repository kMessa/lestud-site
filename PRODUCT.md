# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Municipalities, merchants, artisans, and musicians in and around Narbonne and the South of France. They land on the site to understand what Le Stud does, assess credibility, and decide whether to get in touch. Some are first-time buyers of audiovisual services; others have worked with agencies before and are looking for something less impersonal. Their primary question on arrival: "Can these people do what I need, and can I trust them?"

## Product Purpose

Le Stud is a human-scale audiovisual production studio offering realization, editing, grading, and motion design — from music videos and institutional films to commercial and event content. The site's job is to make that offer immediately legible, to demonstrate quality through the work itself, and to convert interest into contact. Success is a visitor who understands within seconds what Le Stud does and feels confident enough to reach out.

## Brand Personality

Professional, Impactful, Accessible. A real audiovisual studio, but human-sized. Serious without being cold. The confidence of craftsmanship, not of a corporate brochure. Speaks plainly, like someone who knows their job.

## Anti-references

- Generic marketing agency sites: hollow slogans, stock imagery, LinkedIn smiles, "boost your visibility with an innovative digital strategy" copy.
- Startup-fluo aesthetics: purple/blue gradients, neon accents, anything that reads like a 2021 crypto pitch deck.
- Over-minimal sites that are stylish but unintelligible — visitors can't tell what's being sold.
- Flashy effects and heavy animations that prioritize spectacle over content.
- Pretentious agency copy: "we craft the narrative ecosystem of your brand." Nobody talks like that.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the work first.** Real portfolio imagery is the primary proof of quality. No stock photos, no placeholders.
2. **Clear before clever.** The offer is legible in under five seconds. Hierarchy serves comprehension, not aesthetics.
3. **Human-scale confidence.** The tone is a craftsperson who knows their job — not a faceless agency, not a solo freelancer hedging. Direct, warm, unembellished.
4. **Form earns its place.** Every visual effect has a reason. Nothing decorates for its own sake or slows the page.
5. **Speak the client's language.** "We shoot music videos, corporate films, and events" beats "we craft immersive audiovisual experiences." Concrete over abstract, always.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Ensure sufficient contrast across dark and light sections. Reduced-motion preference should disable scroll animations and parallax effects. French language throughout.
